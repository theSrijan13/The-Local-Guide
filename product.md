
# Street Food Recommender - Delhi Local Guide

## About This Project
This is a street food recommendation system for Delhi, India. The system helps users discover the best street food in Delhi based on their preferences, budget, dietary restrictions, and location.

## City Context
- **City**: Delhi (New Delhi)
- **State**: National Capital Territory of Delhi
- **Famous For**: Mughlai cuisine, Chaat culture, Paranthe Wali Gali, 24/7 food scene
- **Street Food Culture**: Delhi is the street food capital of India with influences from Mughlai, Punjabi, and North Indian cuisines. The city never sleeps when it comes to food, with vendors operating at all hours.

## Street Food Database

### 1. Chole Bhature
- **Category**: Main Course
- **Type**: Vegetarian
- **Price Range**: ₹60-100
- **Spice Level**: Medium
- **Description**: Spicy chickpea curry served with large, fluffy deep-fried bread. A Delhi breakfast staple.
- **Best Time**: Breakfast, Lunch (7 AM - 2 PM)
- **Popular Locations**: Sitaram Diwan Chand (Paharganj), Chache Di Hatti (Kamla Nagar), Sindhi Colony (East of Kailash)
- **Local Tip**: Best enjoyed fresh in the morning. Ask for extra onions and pickle.
- **Allergens**: Gluten
- **Serving Style**: 2 bhature with a bowl of chole, onions, and green chutney

### 2. Paranthe (Stuffed Parathas)
- **Category**: Main Course
- **Type**: Vegetarian
- **Price Range**: ₹50-120
- **Spice Level**: Mild to Medium
- **Description**: Crispy, flaky flatbreads stuffed with various fillings - potato, paneer, gobhi, radish, or mixed
- **Best Time**: Breakfast, Lunch
- **Popular Locations**: Paranthe Wali Gali (Chandni Chowk), Moolchand Parantha (South Ex), Giani's (multiple locations)
- **Varieties**: Aloo, Gobhi, Paneer, Mooli, Mixed, Rabri Parantha (sweet)
- **Local Tip**: Order with curd, pickle, and aloo sabzi
- **Allergens**: Gluten, Dairy
- **Serving Style**: Served hot with butter on top

### 3. Gol Gappe (Pani Puri)
- **Category**: Chaat
- **Type**: Vegetarian
- **Price Range**: ₹30-50
- **Spice Level**: Medium to Hot
- **Description**: Crispy hollow puris filled with spiced potato, chickpeas, and tangy tamarind water. Delhi's most addictive snack.
- **Best Time**: Evening (4 PM - 8 PM)
- **Popular Locations**: Bengal Sweet House (Khan Market), Bikanervala (multiple), Prince Paan Corner (CP)
- **Local Names**: Pani Puri, Golgappa, Puchka
- **Varieties**: Meetha paani (sweet), Teekha paani (spicy), Dahi puri (with yogurt)
- **Allergens**: Gluten
- **Serving Style**: Set of 6-8 pieces, eat immediately

## Popular Street Food Zones in Delhi

### Zone 1: Chandni Chowk & Old Delhi
- **Type**: Historical food hub, Mughlai & traditional North Indian
- **Best For**: Paranthe, Nihari, Kebabs, Jalebi, Traditional sweets
- **Peak Hours**: 9 AM - 11 AM (breakfast), 6 PM - 10 PM (dinner)
- **Budget**: ₹50-200 per person
- **Must Try**: Paranthe Wali Gali, Karim's Kebabs, Old Famous Jalebi Wala
- **Getting There**: Chandni Chowk Metro Station (Yellow Line)
- **Local Tip**: Very crowded, best visited on weekday mornings

### Zone 2: Connaught Place (CP)
- **Type**: Mix of street food and established vendors
- **Best For**: Momos, Rolls, Chaat, Fast food
- **Peak Hours**: 5 PM - 10 PM
- **Budget**: ₹100-300 per person
- **Must Try**: Keventers, Prince Paan Corner, Rajinder Da Dhaba
- **Getting There**: Rajiv Chowk Metro Station (Blue & Yellow Line intersection)
- **Local Tip**: Safe and clean, good for first-timers

### Zone 3: Karol Bagh
- **Type**: Dense residential-commercial area with diverse food
- **Best For**: Parathas, Kulfi, North Indian snacks
- **Peak Hours**: 6 PM - 11 PM
- **Budget**: ₹60-150 per person
- **Must Try**: Roshan Di Kulfi, Street vendors on Ajmal Khan Road
- **Getting There**: Karol Bagh Metro Station (Blue Line)
- **Local Tip**: Parking is difficult, use metro

### Zone 4: Majnu Ka Tila (Tibetan Colony)
- **Type**: Tibetan and Chinese food hub
- **Best For**: Momos, Thukpa, Tibetan bread, Laphing
- **Peak Hours**: 12 PM - 9 PM
- **Budget**: ₹100-250 per person
- **Must Try**: AMA Cafe, Ama Kitchen, Dolma Restaurant
- **Getting There**: Vidhan Sabha Metro Station (Yellow Line) + auto
- **Local Tip**: Peaceful vibe, great for authentic Tibetan food

### Zone 5: Khan Market & Nearby
- **Type**: Upscale street food, clean and organized
- **Best For**: Gol Gappe, Rolls, Modern street food
- **Peak Hours**: 5 PM - 9 PM
- **Budget**: ₹150-300 per person
- **Must Try**: Khan Chacha, Bengal Sweet House
- **Getting There**: Khan Market Metro Station (Violet Line)
- **Local Tip**: More expensive but very clean and safe

### Zone 6: Lajpat Nagar Central Market
- **Type**: South Delhi food hub
- **Best For**: Momos, Chaats, Fast food, Kulfi
- **Peak Hours**: 6 PM - 10 PM
- **Budget**: ₹80-200 per person
- **Must Try**: QD's Momos, Street food vendors in main market
- **Getting There**: Lajpat Nagar Metro Station (Violet Line)
- **Local Tip**: Very crowded on weekends, go on weekdays

### Zone 7: Sarojini Nagar Market
- **Type**: Budget shopping + street food
- **Best For**: Quick snacks while shopping, chaats, rolls
- **Peak Hours**: 5 PM - 9 PM
- **Budget**: ₹50-150 per person
- **Must Try**: Various chaat and momo vendors
- **Getting There**: INA Metro Station (Yellow Line) + auto
- **Local Tip**: Combine shopping with street food

### Zone 8: Delhi University North Campus
- **Type**: Student food hub, very affordable
- **Best For**: Budget meals, momos, rolls, maggi, chai
- **Peak Hours**: 4 PM - 10 PM
- **Budget**: ₹50-120 per person
- **Must Try**: Hudson Lane vendors, GTB Nagar food lanes
- **Getting There**: GTB Nagar or Vishwavidyalaya Metro (Yellow Line)
- **Local Tip**: Extremely popular with students, very affordable

### Zone 9: Jama Masjid Area
- **Type**: Mughlai & meat specialties
- **Best For**: Nihari, Kebabs, Mutton dishes, Biryani
- **Peak Hours**: 8 PM - 11 PM (many open after evening prayers)
- **Budget**: ₹150-300 per person
- **Must Try**: Karim's, Al Jawahar, Aslam Chicken, Kallu Nihari
- **Getting There**: Jama Masjid Metro Station (Violet Line)
- **Local Tip**: Many places closed during Ramadan daytime, open late

### Zone 10: Satya Niketan
- **Type**: Student area near DU South Campus
- **Best For**: Budget North Indian meals, Chinese, momos
- **Peak Hours**: 6 PM - 11 PM
- **Budget**: ₹80-150 per person
- **Must Try**: Various small dhabas and cafes
- **Getting There**: Durgabai Deshmukh South Campus Metro (Pink Line)
- **Local Tip**: Very student-friendly prices

## Local Terminology & Delhi Slang

- **"Bhai/Bhaiya"**: Brother (respectful way to address vendors)
- **"Aunty/Uncle"**: Used for middle-aged vendors
- **"Tikha/Teekha"**: Spicy
- **"Meetha"**: Sweet
- **"Chatpata"**: Tangy and spicy (Delhi chaat flavor)
- **"Thanda"**: Cold/Chilled
- **"Garam/Garam Garam"**: Hot (fresh and hot)
- **"Plate/Half Plate"**: Serving portion
- **"Parcel Kar Do"**: Pack it for takeaway
- **"Extra Masala/Chutney"**: More spices/chutney
- **"Bina Pyaaz/Adrak"**: Without onions/ginger
- **"Zara Kam Mirch"**: Less spicy
- **"Fully Loaded"**: With all toppings
- **"Cutting Chai"**: Half cup of tea
- **"One by Two"**: Share one portion between two people
- **"Jugaad"**: Creative solution (Delhi's philosophy!)
- **"Mast"**: Excellent/Great
- **"Kadak"**: Strong (usually for tea) or very spicy

## Important Notes for Delhi Street Food

### Hygiene Tips
- Look for clean preparation areas
- Ensure food is cooked fresh in front of you
- Prefer vendors with good crowd (sign of popularity and turnover)
- Famous established vendors are generally safer
- Avoid raw salads if you have a sensitive stomach
- Look for vendors using filtered water for pani puri

### Payment Methods
- Most accept cash (keep small change!)
- Many popular vendors now accept UPI (Paytm, Google Pay, PhonePe, Bhim)
- Some established places accept cards
- ATMs available near most food zones

### Operating Hours
- Most street vendors: 5 PM - 11 PM
- Old Delhi: Many open from 8 AM onwards
- Breakfast spots: 6 AM - 12 PM
- Late night spots: Open till 2-3 AM
- Some areas: 24/7 (Old Delhi, highway dhabas)

### Transport & Parking
- **Metro is best**: Delhi Metro connects to all major food zones
- Parking is difficult in Old Delhi, CP, Khan Market - use metro
- Auto-rickshaws and Uber/Ola readily available
- Old Delhi: Walk from metro, narrow lanes

### Safety Tips
- Old Delhi can be crowded - keep belongings secure
- Most areas are safe, but be aware in late nights
- Travel in groups in unfamiliar areas after 11 PM
- Women travelers: All recommended areas are generally safe

### Weather Considerations
- **Summer (April-June)**: Very hot, go in evenings, stay hydrated
- **Monsoon (July-September)**: Some vendors close if heavy rain
- **Winter (November-February)**: Best time! Pleasant weather, special items available
- **Peak hours**: Avoid 12-2 PM in summer (too hot)

### Budget Planning
- Budget meal: ₹50-80 per person
- Moderate exploration: ₹150-250 per person
- Food tour experience: ₹300-500 per person
- Weekly budget (eating out daily): ₹2000-3000

### Eating Etiquette
- Most street food is eaten with hands
- Wash hands or use hand sanitizer
- Don't waste food
- Pay immediately or before eating (depends on vendor)
- Tipping: Not mandatory but ₹10-20 appreciated at sit-down places

### Health & Water
- Drink bottled water only
- Carry your own water bottle
- Don't drink tap water or ice from unknown sources
- Digestive issues common for tourists - carry basic medication
- Famous places generally use filtered water

## Final Tips for Street Food in Delhi

✅ **Do's:**
- Start with famous, established vendors
- Go during peak hours (fresh food, safer)
- Use metro for transport
- Carry cash and small change
- Try local favorites, not just tourist spots
- Ask locals for recommendations
- Take photos (vendors usually don't mind)
- Explore beyond Old Delhi

❌ **Don'ts:**
- Don't drink tap water
- Don't eat at completely empty places
- Don't overeat in one go (pace yourself!)
- Don't skip meals to save appetite (you'll get too hungry)
- Don't ignore spice warnings
- Don't park in Old Delhi (too congested)
- Don't visit in extreme summer afternoons

**Remember**: Delhi street food is an experience, not just a meal. Take your time, explore different areas, and enjoy the incredible diversity!
